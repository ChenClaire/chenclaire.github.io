Grovo Explore

A guided tour of Grovo for early stage potential buyers

  Background

1. Early stage potential buyers are often not ready to speak with a salesperson before getting a better understanding of the value of a particular solution. Allowing potential buyers to interact with the product itself is one of the key ways to market and drive leads for SaaS products.

2. Data from Google Analytics shows there is a major drop off once people land on the Learn More page; only 15% of visitors that land on the Learn More page actually submit their information to request a demo.

https://www.grovo.com/learn-more

The Request-A-Demo form is intimidating for early stage potential buyers due to the high number of fields required

  Goal

Design a frictionless and lightweight product tour to:

1. introduce key features to early stage potential buyers and excite them about the product

2. make the product less gated and attract more serious buyers for the Sales team

  Research and findings

We talked to the Sales and Customer Success teams to better understand our potential and current customers by asking the following 2 questions:

What features does a potential buyer look for when evaluating a new learning product?

What features excite our current customers the most?

Based on their feedback, we decided not to open any complicated features that will make potential buyers lose interests. Also, we decided to hightlight Grovo's key differentiators in Grovo Explore:

1. Engaging and immersive lesson player

2. Extensive Microlearning Library

3. Powerful Create tool

At the same time, I also conducted competitive research on product tour.

Here is a collection of different product tours I found: http://bit.ly/2BgBi8Q

There are mainly 2 ways to highlight key features in the product for first-time users:

1. Guided tour: Users follow the step-by-step instructions to go through the highlighted features.

2. Free exploration: Users explore the product on their own pace. Whenever the user is looking at a feature for the the first time, the instructions will show up.

We decided to guide our potential buyers through 3 highlighted features. After the guided tour, they will be able to explore the entire product by themselves.
