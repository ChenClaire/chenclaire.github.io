Grovo Explore

A tour of Grovo for potential buyers

  Problem

Grovo didn't provide an engaging and easy way for potential buyers to get to know about Grovo's Microlearning solution.

1. Requesting a demo was the only way for potential buyers to get a better understanding of Grovo.

But early stage potential buyers are often not ready to speak with a salesperson before getting a better understanding of the value of a particular solution.

2. Request a demo form was too serious and scared away lots of people.

Data from Google Analytics shows there is a major drop off once people land on the Learn More page; only 15% of visitors that land on the Learn More page actually submit their information to request a demo.

https://www.grovo.com/learn-more

The Request-A-Demo form is intimidating for early stage potential buyers due to the high number of fields required

  Goal

Design a frictionless, engaging, and lightweight product tour to:

1. introduce Microlearning solution to potential buyers and differentiate Grovo from the other learning products

2. make the product less gated and attract more serious buyers for the Sales team

  Research and findings

First of all, I conducted competitive research on product tour.

Here is a collection of different product tours I went through: http://bit.ly/2BgBi8Q

And I found out there were mainly 2 types of product tour:

1. Guided tour - Users follow guidence:

Users follow the step-by-step instructions to go through the highlighted features.

2. Free exploration - Guidence follows users:

Users explore the product on their own pace. Whenever the user is looking at a feature for the the first time, the instructions will show up.

To keep the product tour short and engaging, I assumed we shouldn't open the entire product and we should only showcase the key differentiators.

But what features should be showcased? Is my assumption right? I talked to the Sales and Customer Success teams by asking the following questions:

SALES TEAM

What features does a potential buyer look for when evaluating a new learning product?

CUSTOMER SUCCESS TEAM

What features excite our current customers the most?

These two teams gave me the exact same answers:

1. Watching Microlearning lessons

2. Browsing Microlearning Library

3. Creating Microlearning content

And Sales team velidated my assumption as well: no need to open the entire product to potential buyers. Because Sales team does a great job at introducing complicated features (e.g. user management and reporting,) to potential buyer, and it's very possible that people will lost their interest when trying these complicated features by themselves.
